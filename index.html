Section 2: Fun & Easy Experiments üß™

Time to put on your lab coat! Choose an experiment below to see the instructions. Remember to ask an adult for help!

You Will Need:

  • A small plastic bottle
  • Play-doh or clay
  • 2 tablespoons of baking soda
  • 1/2 cup of vinegar
  • A few drops of red food coloring (optional)
  • A tray or pan to contain the "lava"

Instructions:

  1. Build Your Volcano: Place the bottle on the tray. Use play-doh to build a volcano shape around it.
  2. Add the "Magma": Pour baking soda and food coloring into the bottle.
  3. Time to Erupt! Quickly pour in the vinegar and watch the fizzy lava flow!

What's Happening? When baking soda and vinegar mix, they create a gas called carbon dioxide. The gas builds up pressure and erupts out, just like a real volcano!

You Will Need:

  • A tall, clear glass
  • Water
  • An egg
  • 6 tablespoons of salt

Instructions:

  1. Fill the glass halfway with water. Gently place the egg in. It sinks!
  2. Take the egg out. Stir in the salt until it dissolves.
  3. Now, gently place the egg back in the saltwater. It floats!

What's Happening? Salt makes water denser (heavier). The salty water is now denser than the egg, which allows the egg to float on top!
